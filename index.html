Our Story

A neighborhood institution, one plate at a time.

Long before "third wave" coffee and reservation apps, there was a diner on 2nd Avenue with a griddle that never went cold. John's opened in the early 1980s with one idea: feed people well, treat them like neighbors, and never run out of coffee. Forty-plus years later, that's still the whole plan.

Regulars know the rhythm — counter seats fill up first, the kitchen moves fast, and someone always asks "the usual?" before you've even sat down. Travelers staying near the UN and Midtown East find us by accident and come back on purpose. The menu gets the occasional refresh, but the soul of the place — paper checks, a "morning!" from whoever's behind the counter, and that famous green chili sauce — hasn't changed a bit.
